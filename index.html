Multimedia

This course provides an opportunity to learn both Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop. Students will explore menus, palettes, and tools in Illustrator. Projects include computer illustrations, CD covers, photo-realistic art, logo design, editorial illustration, and cartooning.In Photoshop, Students will learn to use tools, menus, filters, painting, and color modification. They will discover how to effectively use masking and advanced layering techniques to further manipulate images. Projects include image montages, posters, surrealist images, self-portraits, web banners, Photoshop effects, photo-realistic art and much more.

Design & Drawing

DDP provides students with opportunities to apply creative thinking, decision-making and problem solving skills to develop solutions to design problems. It utilizes powerful computer hardware and software (AutoDesk Inventor) to develop 3D models or solid renderings of objects. This computer-based process replaces traditional board drawing methods while incorporating all of the concepts and skills of previous courses, including hand sketching. Student will learn the product design process and how a model of that product is created, analyzed, rendered and produced. Various applications of the product design process will be discussed along with possible career opportunities.

Video

This course is designed to introduce students to the world of video production, video editing and basic animation. Topics include: story-boarding, script writing, acting, video techniques and managing audio. Collaborative projects like stop motion animation, claymation, music videos, commercials and documentaries provide students with hands on experience in basic production. At the conclusion of the course students will create a online webpage portfolio which is a compilation of their best work. Full color printers and digital cameras are supplied in this state-of-the-art Mac Media Lab. Students utilize various software that includes; Adobe Photoshop, iMovie, iTunes iPhoto and Garage Band.
